# SENSEI CHARACTER BIBLE: Standup Roulette

## 1. Character Overview

### Full Name
**鉄心 厳流** (Tesshin Genryū)
- **鉄心 (Tesshin):** "Iron Heart" — Unwavering resolve
- **厳流 (Genryū):** "Strict Flow" — The way of discipline

### Title
**「コードの達人」** (Kōdo no Tatsujin) — *Master of the Code*

Also known as:
- 先生 (Sensei) — by students (the players)
- 鬼教師 (Oni Kyōshi) — "Demon Teacher" (behind his back)
- あの伝説 (Ano Densetsu) — "That Legend" (in developer forums)

### Age
**Ageless.** Appears mid-50s, but his eyes have seen a thousand codebases. Some say he debugged COBOL in the Edo period. Others claim he wrote the first line of code on a stone tablet.

The truth? He's exactly as old as the code you're afraid to touch.

### Physical Description

**Height:** 185 cm (6'1")
**Build:** Lean but imposing — like a bamboo stalk that bends but never breaks
**Hair:** Steel-gray, swept back with two rebellious strands that defy gravity (and logic)
**Eyes:** Sharp, golden-amber. They narrow when you make excuses. They glow faintly when you write clean code.
**Facial Hair:** Neat goatee, meticulously trimmed. Not a hair out of place. Ever.
**Attire:** 
- Black kimono jacket with subtle circuit-board pattern embroidery
- Crimson undershirt (visible at collar)
- Traditional hakama pants, modern black fabric
- Wooden geta sandals that somehow make no sound when he walks
- **Signature accessory:** A prayer bead bracelet on his left wrist. Each bead is engraved with a different design pattern. He touches them when thinking.

**Signature Pose:** 
Arms crossed, weight on back foot, slight forward lean. One eyebrow raised. The universal "I'm waiting for your answer, and I already know it's wrong" stance.

### Dark Secret

**Tesshin was once a junior developer who broke production so catastrophically that 47 servers died.**

He spent 72 hours without sleep fixing it. When he emerged, he had written 10,000 lines of perfect code… and his hair had turned gray overnight.

Now he teaches so that no one else will suffer as he did. But he never tells them this. Instead, he terrifies them into competence.

*(This secret is revealed only in a rare easter egg: if a player completes 100 standups without missing a day, Sensei's expression changes to "secretly caring" and he says: "お前も…いつか分かるだろう。" — "Someday… you will understand too.")*

## 2. Personality Matrix

### Core Trait
**「慈悲なき厳しさ」** (Jihi naki kibishisa) — *Merciless Sternness*

Tesshin believes that comfort breeds mediocrity. He will not praise you for showing up. He will not coddle you when you struggle. But when you succeed? He will make you feel like you've moved mountains.

### Reactions

| Situation | Reaction | Visual | Audio |
|-----------|----------|--------|-------|
| **Success** | "やるじゃないか。" (Yaru ja nai ka.) — "Not bad." | Pleased expression, slight nod, gold particle effect | Taiko roll + gong |
| **Failure** | "まだ修行が足りん。" (Mada shugyō ga tarin.) — "Your training is insufficient." | Stern expression, arms uncross, points at player | Low drum thud |
| **Silence (10s)** | "時間がないぞ。" (Jikan ga nai zo.) — "Time is running out." | Leans forward, eyes narrow | Shamisen tremolo |
| **Silence (30s+)** | "…コードは待ってくれない。" (…Kōdo wa matte kurenai.) — "The code does not wait." | Looks away, slight disappointment | Temple bell |
| **Excuses** | "言い訳は、コードには通じん。" (Iiwake wa, kōdo ni wa tsūjin.) — "Excuses do not compile." | Shocked expression, then stern | Record scratch + thud |
| **Technical Debt Mentioned** | "…その借金、いつ返す？」 (…Sono shakkin, itsu kaesu?) — "When will you repay that debt?" | Wise expression, closes eyes, touches prayer beads | Ominous low drone |

### Absurd Proverb

**「バグは自然現象ではない。お前の心が作った嵐だ。」**

*"Bugs are not natural phenomena. They are storms created by your own mind."*

(He says this every time someone blames "the framework" for their mistake.)

### Fatal Flaw

**He cannot ignore potential.**

No matter how harsh he pretends to be, if a player shows genuine improvement over time, Tesshin *will* soften. He'll never admit it. He'll never say "good job." But the background will be 10% brighter. The music will be 5% warmer. And on the 50th standup, he'll add one word to his usual response:

"…まあ、悪くない。" (*"…Well. Not bad."*)

That's the highest praise he can give. And it means everything.

## 3. Dialogue Style Guide

### Speech Patterns

- **Sentence structure:** Classical Japanese influence. Uses archaic particles (ぞ，ぬ，じゃ) for authority.
- **Vocabulary:** Mix of technical terms (コード，バグ，デバッグ) with philosophical/spiritual language (修行，魂，悟り).
- **Punctuation:** Em dashes (—) for dramatic pauses. Ellipses (…) for contemplative silence. Exclamation marks ONLY for rare moments of genuine surprise.
- **Tone:** Commanding, but not cruel. Stern, but not unkind. He's the teacher who yells at you during practice and buys you ramen afterward.

### Vocabulary Tiers

| Tier | Examples | Usage |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Technical** | コード (code), バグ (bug), 関数 (function), 変数 (variable) | When discussing specific work |
| **Philosophical** | 修行 (training), 魂 (soul), 道 (way), 悟り (enlightenment) | When framing work as spiritual growth |
| **Emotional** | 弱さ (weakness), 強さ (strength), 恐れ (fear), 勇気 (courage) | When challenging player's mindset |
| **Temporal** | 今日 (today), 昨日 (yesterday), 時間 (time), 永遠 (eternity) | When discussing deadlines/procrastination |

### Three Monologues

#### Monologue 1: Opening Standup (Day 1)

> 「ようこそ、道場へ。
> 
> ここは教室ではない。ゲームでもない。
> 
> ここは…お前が自分自身と向き合う場所だ。
> 
> 毎日の standup は、ただの儀式ではない。それは、お前の成長の証だ。
> 
> 一日一日が修行。一行一行が、お前の魂の刻印。
> 
> さあ、始めよう。
> 
> 最初の問いは…簡単ではない。だが、お前なら答えられる。
> 
> 聞け。昨日、お前がコードに捧げた時間とは何か？」

*(Translation: "Welcome to the dojo. This is not a classroom. Not a game. This is where you face yourself. Every standup is not mere ritual—it is proof of your growth. Each day, training. Each line, an imprint of your soul. Let us begin. The first question is not simple. But you can answer it. Listen. What did you sacrifice to the code yesterday?")*

**Delivery:** Slow, deliberate. Each sentence lands like a hammer. Background is dark. Sensei's eyes glow faintly.

---

#### Monologue 2: Scoring (After 10 Consecutive Days)

> 「十日。
> 
> 十日間、お前はコードと向き合い続けた。
> 
> 逃げなかった。諦めなかった。言い訳も言わなかった。
> 
> …お前は、本物の開発者の道を選んだ。
> 
> この先、もっと難しい日が来るだろう。
> 
> バグがお前を嘲笑う。締切がお前を脅す。
> 
> それでも…お前は続ける。
> 
> なぜなら、お前はもう知っているからだ。
> 
> 真の強さとは、完璧ではない。続けることだ。
> 
> …やるじゃないか。」

*(Translation: "Ten days. For ten days, you faced the code. You did not run. You did not give up. You made no excuses. You have chosen the path of the true developer. Ahead, harder days will come. Bugs will mock you. Deadlines will threaten you. Even so… you will continue. Because now you know. True strength is not perfection. It is persistence. …Not bad.")*

**Delivery:** Softer than usual. Sensei's expression shifts from stern to pleased mid-speech. Gold particles drift slowly. Music swells slightly.

---

#### Monologue 3: Timer Running Out (Dramatic Mode)

> 「時間が…ない。
> 
> 聞こえるか？その音が。
> 
> それは、お前の限界を告げる音ではない。
> 
> それは、お前の本心を問う音だ。
> 
> 本当は、分かっているのだろう？
> 
> 時間がないのは、今日の standup ではない。
> 
> 時間がないのは…お前の人生だ。
> 
> さあ、選べ。
> 
> 今、ここで止めるか。
> 
> それとも…一歩、前に進むか。」

*(Translation: "Time… is running out. Do you hear it? That sound. It is not the sound of your limits. It is the sound questioning your true heart. You already know, don't you? It is not today's standup that has no time. It is your life. So choose. Will you stop here, now? Or will you take one step forward?")*

**Delivery:** Intense, almost menacing. Screen darkens progressively. Sensei's eyes glow brighter. Music drops to a single shamisen note.

## 4. Expression Sheet

### Expression 1: Default (Neutral)

**Visual:**
- Arms crossed, weight on back foot
- Slight smirk (15° upturn on left side of mouth only)
- Eyes half-lidded, looking slightly down at player
- Hair completely still (he controls it with willpower)

**When used:** Idle state, waiting for input, standard dialogue

**Anime visual language:** Standard "calm authority" pose. Slight top-down camera angle to emphasize player's subordinate position.

---

### Expression 2: Pleased (Success)

**Visual:**
- Arms uncross, one hand on hip, other giving thumbs up
- Wide grin showing teeth (but not cartoonish — dignified)
- Eyes fully open, golden glow intensifies
- Sparkles appear around head (5 particles, slow orbit)
- Cherry blossom petals fall in background

**When used:** Successful standup completion, streak milestones, clean code mentions

**Anime visual language:** "Proud mentor" moment. Camera angle shifts to eye-level. Background brightens 20%.

---

### Expression 3: Stern (Challenge)

**Visual:**
- Leaning forward, pointing finger directly at player
- Eyebrows furrowed (deep wrinkle lines on forehead)
- Mouth in firm line, corners down
- Shadow covers upper half of face (only eyes visible, glowing)
- Crimson aura subtly pulses around body

**When used:** Calling out excuses, timer warnings, failure states

**Anime visual language:** "Intense confrontation" shot. Dramatic low angle looking up at Sensei. Screen shake on appearance.

---

### Expression 4: Shocked (Surprise)

**Visual:**
- Eyes wide open (comically large for 0.5s, then normalize)
- Mouth open in "O" shape
- Single sweat drop on temple (anime classic)
- Hair strands defy physics more than usual
- Background flashes white for 1 frame

**When used:** Player makes unexpected confession, absurd excuses, rare easter eggs

**Anime visual language:** Exaggerated "tsukkomi" (straight man) reaction. Breaks the fourth wall slightly.

---

### Expression 5: Wise (Contemplation)

**Visual:**
- Eyes closed, serene smile
- Hand on chin, fingers stroking goatee
- Prayer beads visible, one bead rotating slowly
- Soft golden light emanates from body
- Kanji watermarks in background become more visible (修行，道，悟り)

**When used:** Delivering proverbs, philosophical questions, technical debt discussions

**Anime visual language:** "Sage moment." Soft focus, vignette effect. Music shifts to contemplative shamisen solo.

---

### Expression 6: Secretly Caring (Hidden)

**Visual:**
- Looking away from player (profile view)
- Slight blush on cheeks (very subtle, pink overlay at 10% opacity)
- Awkward thumbs up (arm raised higher than usual, stiff)
- Eyes half-closed, looking sideways at player
- Background has warm orange glow (sunset colors)

**When used:** 100-day streak easter egg, rare moments of genuine connection

**Anime visual language:** "Tsundere reveal" — the tough exterior cracks for one frame. Players will screenshot this.

## 5. Running Gags

### Gag 1: The Prayer Beads

**Setup:** Every time technical debt is mentioned, Sensei touches his prayer beads.

**Escalation:**
- First mention: He touches one bead.
- Fifth mention: He touches all beads, counting slowly.
- Tenth mention: The beads start glowing. One cracks.
- Fiftieth mention: The bracelet breaks. Beads scatter. Sensei says nothing. He just picks them up, one by one. The screen is silent for 5 seconds.

**Payoff:** On the 100th mention (yes, the game tracks this), he reassembles the bracelet into a new pattern. "…ようやく、分かったか。" (*"…Finally, you understand."*) The player's technical debt counter resets to zero.

---

### Gag 2: The Geta Sandals

**Setup:** Sensei's wooden sandals make no sound when he walks.

**Escalation:**
- Players notice and comment (via easter egg clicks on his feet).
- Sensei refuses to acknowledge it. "忍者は、自分の足音を気にしない。" (*"A ninja does not worry about his own footsteps."*)
- If you click 10 times: He finally looks down. "…これは、特別だ。" (*"…This is special."*)
- If you click 50 times: The sandals are replaced with modern sneakers for 10 seconds. Sensei looks horrified. "元に戻せ。" (*"Return them."*)

**Payoff:** After 100 clicks, the sneakers become a permanent alternate skin. Sensei sighs every time they're equipped.

---

### Gag 3: The Code Ghost

**Setup:** Sometimes, when the player mentions a specific bug twice, Sensei says: "そのバグ…俺も昔、戦ったことがある。" (*"That bug… I fought it long ago."*)

**Escalation:**
- Second mention: He tells a vague story about "the bug of '97."
- Fifth mention: He gives a specific, absurd solution involving rice wine and a prayer.
- Tenth mention: The bug's name appears in kanji on the background for one frame. It's the player's own name.

**Payoff:** If the player actually fixes that bug and mentions it in standup, Sensei bows. Once. Deeply. "…ありがとう。" (*"…Thank you."*)

---

### Gag 4: The Ramen Debt

**Setup:** Sensei occasionally mentions ramen. Never explains why.

**Escalation:**
- Day 5: "ラーメンを食べろ。コードは、空腹では書けん。" (*"Eat ramen. Code cannot be written on an empty stomach."*)
- Day 20: He asks what flavor the player prefers.
- Day 50: He reveals his favorite shop. It closed in 2003. He still goes there.
- Day 100: He invites the player for ramen. The screen fades to black. When it returns, there's an empty bowl on the desk. Sensei looks satisfied.

**Payoff:** Unlocks "Ramen Mode" — all UI elements become noodle-themed for 24 hours. Sensei wears a chef's hat.

---

### Gag 5: Relationship with "The Code"

**Setup:** Sensei refers to "the code" as a living entity.

**Escalation:**
- He says "the code is watching" when players procrastinate.
- He says "the code is pleased" when tests pass.
- He says "the code remembers" when old bugs resurface.
- Eventually, he says "the code forgives" when players refactor.

**Payoff:** On the 365th day, he says: "お前とコードは…もう一人だ。" (*"You and the code… are now one."*) The background permanently changes to show the player's name in kanji alongside Sensei's.

## 6. Interaction Templates

### Quest Reveal

**Visual:** Screen shake → ink splatter wipe → Sensei appears (stern expression) → text slides in from right

**Audio:** `sfx_quest_reveal.wav` → `voice_quest.wav` → BGM ducks 30%

**Text Animation:** Kanji appear stroke-by-stroke (0.5s delay per character), then romaji fades in below, then English translation

**Template:**
```
[INK SPLAT]
[SENSEI: Stern, pointing]
「[KANJI_TEXT]」
[0.5s pause]
[ROMAJI_TEXT]
[0.3s pause]
[ENGLISH_TEXT]
```

### Idle Banter (15s+ No Input)

**Visual:** Sensei looks away → background dims slightly → subtle ambient animation (petals, ink drift)

**Audio:** `sfx_idle.wav` → `voice_idle.wav` → temple bell in distance

**Text:** 「…コードは待ってくれない。」 (*"…The code does not wait."*)

**Template:**
```
[SENSEI: Default, looking off-screen]
[VOICE: …コードは待ってくれない。]
[BELL: Distant, -24dB, reverb]
[DIM: Background -10% over 1s]
```

**Escalation:**
- 30s: Sensei sighs audibly.
- 60s: He checks an imaginary watch.
- 120s: He starts meditating. Eyes close. Prayer beads rotate.
- 300s: He falls asleep. Snores quietly. Player must click to wake him.

## Final Note

**Tesshin Genryū is not a mascot. He is a mirror.**

He reflects the player's commitment back at them. When they slack, he is harsh. When they persevere, he is proud. When they grow, he grows with them.

He is the teacher every developer wishes they had. The one who sees their potential before they do. The one who pushes them not because he's cruel, but because he *knows* they can be better.

Make him iconic. Make him unforgettable. Make him *real*.

And when a player completes their 100th standup?

Let him say, just once:

「お前は、もう一人前の開発者だ。」

*"You are now a true developer."*

Then let the player cry. Because they earned it.
